Sri Lanka has entered a phase of rapid growth and development, which requires a healthy workforce, which is physically and intellectually able to meet current and future challenges. The health of an individual and thereby a nation primarily depends significantly on nutrition, among other factors.

It is quite well known that Sri Lanka’s health indicators are among the best in the region. Particularly, human development indicators in Sri Lanka show significant improvements and are comparatively better than those of other South Asian countries. According to statistics from the Central Bank the average life expectancy is 74.9years(2011). The literacy rate of the country is 91.9% (2010), on par if not higher than even in some developed countries. The maternal mortality rate is 31 per 100,000 live births (2010) and infant mortality 9 per 1,000 live births (2009) However, for some unknown reason the nutrition indicators do not match those of health; the nutritional status of children, women and adolescents have remained stagnant for over a decade and do not match with the present levels of socio economic development.

In the recent past (1999), there was a change in the countries policy where the subject of nutrition was brought under the Ministry of Health from the Ministry of Plan Implementation.Further, recent data from the National Micro Nutrient Survey (2012) of the Medical Research Institute indicates as illustrated thatstunting has decreased while wasting has increased since 2009 and calls for urgent action.

However the improvement of nutrition needs interventions from many sectors, not only health, and therefore is recognised as requiring a multi-sector approach.

As such, in keeping with the recommendationscontained in the National Nutrition Policy (2009 – 2013) it was proposed to have a National Nutrition Council (NNC) functioning directly under the leadership of His Excellency the President, whichallows for better coordination ofnutrition related activities of all relevant ministries, according to the multi-sector approach.

His Excellency the President, thereby having recognised the importance of addressing the current nutrition situation, launched a special programme titled

indicating the priority given to Nutrition by the current Government and commissioned the NNC to develop a suitable plan to achieve this vision supported by the National Nutrition Secretariat of Sri Lanka.

The NNC, which decides on nutrition policy and is chaired by His Excellency the President, consists of relevant Ministers, Chief Ministers of the nine Provinces and Members of Parliament from all parties who have experience in nutrition.

It is supported by; 

     National Steering Committee on Nutrition (NSCN) 

The NSCN is the implementation arm of the NNC and comprises Secretaries of the Ministries represented at the NNC,Chief Secretaries of Provinces, & Civil Society Representatives.International Agencies are invited as observers to the NSCN. 

           Technical Advisory Committee on Nutrition (TACN) 

The TACN provides technical guidance and supports the formulation of policies and plans and comprises subject professionals and experts from various related fields including those from international agencies. The TACN also advisesinmonitoring and evaluation. 

           National Nutrition Secretariat of Sri Lanka (NNSSL)

       The NNSSL is established within the Presidential Secretariat and supports the formulation of plans, coordinates activities amongimplementing parties and monitors and evaluates the progress of implementation on behalf of the National Nutrition Council.The NNSSL enlists the support of international agencies to provide technical assistance and financing as and when needed.

One of the first and primary tasks of the NNSSL was to develop a targeting key priority areas for action with inbuilt monitoring systems,coordinating collaboration among key Ministries in orderto scale-up, combine and targetnutrition actions across Sri Lanka.

A series of consultations have been undertaken with experts and key stakeholders in order to develop theMsAPNwhich complements the National Nutrition Policy (2009 – 2013) and the accompanying National Nutrition Strategic Plan by utilising a multi-sector approach.

This MsAPN,onceapproved by the cabinet of Ministers, will ultimately be endorsed by His Excellency the President and will be implemented over a period of three years from 2013 to 2016.
 

  • developa multi sector action plan with an inbuilt monitoring system 
  • supportcapacity building on nutrition at all levels
  • implement an integrated social marketing campaign on nutrition
  • conductregular reviews with relevant Ministries regarding progress of implementation of the MsAPN actions
  • monitor and evaluatethe progress of activities of the MsAPN at National, Provincial, District and Divisional levels
  • conduct nutrition surveillance at regular intervals
  • update the National Nutrition Council on the progress of nutrition activities in the country.

 The NNSSL remains committed to ensure that the MsAPN will be effective in realising that Sri Lanka does become A Nourished  Nation by 2016.
